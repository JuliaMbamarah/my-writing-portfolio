Tips for Finding a Good Apartment for Rent

Date: November 1, 2023 | Author: Julia Mbamarah

Finding a good apartment or house to rent may be difficult and time-consuming. Since there is a lot of competition, it might be challenging to locate a decent apartment in a decent location.

Many real estate agents will emphasize the importance of being well-organized. A lot of individuals who are in the market for a rental property put off starting their search until the very last minute. Metro Home Managers is a full-service rental property management company based in Washington, DC, and its co-founder Lance Macon has noticed that renters who are in a rush are more willing to accept less-than-ideal living situations.

Here is a list of nine tried-and-trusted methods that have helped a lot of people make the best decisions when it comes to rentals.

Start Searching Early

If you want to find the most suitable rental considering price, location, and facilities, don’t wait till the last minute. Because rental properties are in less supply, it's recommended to start your search at least 2 months before you need to move. A good tip here is to start your search within the first week of each month since there will be fewer purchasers and more options in the market. The second and third weeks of the month are relatively busier than any other week.

Check Rental Listings Online

If you are looking for a new location to call home, researching the area online through apartment-seeking services is a great idea. To find an apartment, it’s common to have very specific needs, like a set number of bedrooms, or much broader ones. Find out the cost of living in various regions and choose where you would be content depending on the facilities available. It would be worthy of note, however, that there isn't nearly enough information on apartment rental websites on the various areas and the facilities they contain.

Get a Real Estate Agent

Many real estate brokers will not charge fees to help renters locate a suitable property. Consider getting a broker that focuses on rental properties rather than home sales. It might be in your best interest to consult with many real estate agents due to the potentially stiff competition in the rental market. Check to see whether the real estate agent you intend to work with has access to the buildings in the neighborhood you're interested in.

Avoid Being Scammed

Keep in mind that certain internet ads may try to scam you by asking for credit card information as a means of deposit before they can show you apartments for rent. This is an obvious scam as you don’t need to pay any deposit to be shown around. Also, beware of renting from a private party, as this may require you to provide personal information such as your Social Security number, bank account details, and job history to a stranger who will almost definitely run a credit check on you. You should only engage with a real estate broker who has all the required licenses and insurance coverage.

Consider Roommates

It's a good idea to share an apartment with friends or even strangers. It may be worth it to ask the landlord for separate leases if you're worried about your compatibility. For instance, if your monthly rent is $2,000 and your roommates are unable to meet the payment deadline, you’ll be responsible for the entire amount. If, on the other hand, you have a separate lease, you need just pay your portion of the rent. Be sure to ask people you meet for recommendations on housemates if you don't already have buddies living in the region. You may use social networking sites like Facebook, Twitter, and LinkedIn to discover more people living in that region.

Explore Smaller Apartments

A one-bedroom apartment in a building with 100 apartments would cost significantly more than the same-sized unit in a building with 4 to 5 units. This is because bigger buildings incur additional costs due to managing more amenities like swimming pools, lounges, and gyms.

Take Advantage of Social Media

You can let your network know that you are house searching on social media. Spread the word that you're on the hunt for a new place and people will not hesitate to help out where they can.

Don’t Make Decisions Based on Perceived Value Alone

An apartment with desirable features may not be the ideal choice if it is too far from your workplace or lacks other features you require. It is disadvantageous to rent such an apartment even if the rent seems affordable from other people’s perspective, especially when your needs are different from theirs.

Contact Authorities in the Preferred Region First

Always call first to have a feel of the budget before driving out to the property. When you visit the building, the leasing agent may attempt to impress you with a long list of its advantages. When you phone, however, they'll just give you the facts. Check the information obtained from authorities against what you had seen online.

After you've determined your needs and the associated costs, your first step should be to note your present financial status. Consider the impact on your finances on your choices of apartments. Secondly, assign monetary values to the apartment unit features and use the overall value as a basis for negotiations with prospective renters.
