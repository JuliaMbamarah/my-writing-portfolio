Brian’s Biography

For me, living in the US for over 40years as a Black American comes with it’s own level of awareness. There comes a time in an imperfect societal system where a level of realization is attained and life then depends on a much deeper course than all the elements surrounding our today’s world.

Every point of societal construct right from the home, are designed to make us constantly focused on our careers and relationship with our external world. More frequently than not, our fixation on these leaves us so absorbed that we fail to identify tensions that rises or have always existed in our dealings- like Racial Discrimination. And because we cannot classify this tension, it creeps into our existence and affects everything about us. It seeps in to the system that we have created and with it comes self doubt and hypersensitivity to feedbacks.

For a very long time I was unaware of it’s existence, but it still worked a good number on me, making me question my own feelings and agree that since and anytime there was a problem, it must be of my own making. And with this realization came a revelation, where I no longer see myself as a victim of circumstance but rather as a point of contact to society. Individually, we may not be able to control the way society responds to us, but it is left for us to understand it’s intent, question all the stories we hear about ourselves and separate the internal factors affecting is as individuals from the external factors.

I am Brian and this is just the beginning of my story.

Sandra's Biography

So my life is like this: I’ve been basically trolling all my life, now trolling here means living through life, with no real purpose, like a remote controlled person, like a puppet. My actions were based on parent’s instructions, basic life rules. I was just living, unaware, well until reality struck.

I got into the University last year November, where I was expected to take major decision on my own. I had to do this and do that, things I barely felt comfortable doing alone. So basically life in the University toughened me up to an extent, and subsequently I found purpose in life. Maybe not purpose, but at least now, I’m no longer trolling; I know what I want, when and how to get it. I know who I am or at least I’m in the process. I try to develop my person by reading these developmental and inspirational books, I take the info I need, and not just absorb everything.

One of my major projects relating to person now in life is something I discovered myself through critical analysis and rational thinking, that thing is “being yourself”, I’m not going to say much on this but I believe that everyone should try to be themselves as much as possible. It’s from being yourself that you discover who you are and self-discovery is essentially the first step to an effective personal development.

Jane's Biography

I am from a small but culturally aware part of the world where people have strong and varying opinions about different circumstances and occurrences. In my part of the world, who you are doesn't matter when your voice is singing a profound tune.

I am just a girl with dreams of a more realistic world where we don't hide under facades that we have consciously or otherwise have built in years past. Gender inequality as well as gender marginalization is ever evident in my everyday life and it's true that they are variations to how it affects women all over the world, some worse than others.

I believe that if we as global villagers genuinely work together to eliminate these vices, together we can make the world one step closer to a better place for all.
