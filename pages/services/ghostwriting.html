Pitfalls of his life

Chapter One - Maroon

He walked in, more like he staggered in, running into the wooden table, displacing it slightly and causing a sound which would have not been so shrill if it were to have been bereft of the night, followed first by the clinking sounds of glasses running into each other and then finally a shattering sound which pierced deeply into the quiet of the night.

He paused as if for a second realizing what had happened, and then slumped forcefully to the ground, lucky to have landed on the arm of the sofa. He smiled widely and foolishly as if he had just won a lottery, a lottery here of good sleep.

“Goodness me! Eric!”, A lady voice called out in something in between a disgust and a surprise.

Felicia Madonna, highly beautiful, elegant, sophisticated and always contained one could barely see her in a state of distortion. Barely except when it came to her only son and real heir to the fortune of the old man. She was the wife to the late Mayor of the town of Geratine.

“ Eric!! This is the height of it”, she mumbled walking hastily and haphazardly towards the couch where Eric laid with his arms and legs spread out widely. She stopped to get hold of a book lying on the middle glass table, and then hit him with it heavily on the head.

“Owwww! What’s do you waant muuuummm, go awayyyy, I just miss Dad”, he managed to slur out.

Felicia’s frown-tight face slowly sagged, turning to a sad one. She sighed deeply and sat down on the couch just next to the one in which Eric laid. She thought sadly and deeply. It had been two years since that ghastly accident that claimed the life of her husband.

It had been an Easter Sunday, and he had gone in his new Peugeot 104 together with Eric to buy things for dinner and have some father and son moment. She had been at home preparing things for their dinner and decorating the house. Just like every other Easter, it was bound to have been perfect. But why she had woken up having the weirdest vibes that morning is a story she knows now all too well. She realized something had gone wrong after waiting for another half-hour as to the time they were to have arrived.

She’d picked up her cell phone and a bunch of keys, alerted their closest neighbor, and left in the other slightly rickety car they possessed, being thankful they hadn’t given it out yet. She had barely driven out of the street when she got a call. Her heart had skipped a beat when she saw her husband’s name on the Caller’s ID. It skipped even more beats when she had heard a strange voice at the end of the line.

The last thing she had remembered was crying, and like lightning, she had been at the scene of the accident. She had prayed and hoped earnestly that the body being carted away to the ambulance had nothing to do with her. She walked hastily, highly unstably, crying profusely to the cart being pulled away. She couldn’t bring herself to raise the sheets, but when she eventually did, she fell on her knees and cried like she never had, being consoled by passersby who had stopped to catch a view of the scene.

Apparently, there had been an accident, an oncoming trailer lost control and hit, more like squashed the Peugeot completely. When the bodies were recovered, her husband had been dead, while the boy was injured badly.

The boy? Her son!!!

She jumped up quickly just in time to see her son being carted away on a similar-looking stretcher. He was alive!! She sprung towards him. There was a glint of hope in her eyes, the tiniest bit of gladness in her heart.

But when she looked into his, she saw a lifeless gaze, a gaze so distant, a gaze so cold…

Scars to her Beautiful

Chapter One

The first thing that had caught my attention as he walked into the classroom was his neck chain. Its round pendant had a crescent shape inscribed on it. It shone a dazzling silver, reflecting bright light as it caught at an angle. I had the same neck chain, only the inscription on mine was that of a full moon.

"Not so fast, you have to introduce yourself to the class," Obiora had said.

Obiora, once a timid, however brainy boy, was now the class monitor. He came back last two summer breaks transformed, leaving everyone in awe as to how. Our class teacher had no choice but to hand him the position of the class monitor, seeing how academic he was in addition to his newfound charisma.

"Excuse me?" The new boy said, raising his perfectly arched brows as he stopped on his track just in front of the whole class.

His skin blended surreptitiously with the wooden coffee-black furniture that graced the classroom. As he spoke, the auburn of his lips parted to reveal caramel white teeth. His broad shoulders housed a small black backpack that hung lazily from his right, slightly swinging back and forth across the side of his abs, which were ever so evident through the thin ash-colored shirt he wore. His head hung rather firmly over his neck, assuming the same level as that of the top of our whiteboard.

However, like a pot of a mouth-watering delicacy infected with a drop of kerosene, or more so like a dot of black on a white canvas, he had this stony look to him, one that seemed to want to hide his pretty. Fortunately, it wasn't working.

Obiora cleared his throat this time. "I said you have to introduce yourself to the class before you settle down."

From all indications, Obiora was frightened. He was taking deep breaths more frequently than was normal. I give it to him for maintaining his composure and not stuttering on his words. The person in front of the class was terrifying. Although what most people found terrifying, I found intriguing. Amusing even.

"Is it written somewhere in this handbook? They made me read it twice and I'm pretty much sure, I didn't come across anything of such," he said nonchalantly, earning snickers from most students. Even I found it amusing, although I didn't show it. However, his expression was still as stony as the time when he had walked into the class. Angry much.

I went on with my accessing session, gazing yet again at the roughly patched dark auburn lips that had just spoken down to his chest. The long-sleeved t-shirt clung tightly to his shoulders, arms, and torso, revealing the muscles within.

It wasn't a bad sight.

I could make out Obiora still breathing in deeply from my side vision, but now with a furious look. He wasn't enjoying the episode and had obviously had enough. I anticipated his next line of action.

"No, it's not written in the handbook, but it's only courteous and proper that before you walk into an unfamiliar arena that you're going to be constantly walking into for the next couple of months, the least you could do, and mind you I said least, is introduce yourself. It may not be in the rule book, but trust me, the teachers are well aware of it, you could decide to see it as a tradition that definitely you won't be the first to break," he poured out, raising his voice gradually as he spoke and gasped for air once he had been done.

Boom! Mystery boy actually widened his eyes a bit but returned it to normal just as soon, no one else would have noticed. Well, except me. I'm pretty observant. I could see the tiniest bit of impression on his stony face.

"The name's Jeremy," he finally admitted and continued on his track into the already crowded classroom. It was normal for new students to show up to the senior secondary class of students writing WAEC, especially in the first trimester of the academic term.

"Just Jeremy? Okay, let's decide to accept that. So what school had you been transferred from?" Obiora continued. Now as innocent as this question may seem, people ask it for various reasons. One; they just really want to know where you'd been transferred from. Two; it gives them a feeling of superiority knowing what great school you'd left behind to be part of them. The more popular your school had been, the more pleasant the feeling. Three; They want to measure your worth from the school you'd last been at. So believe it or not, there exists a theme in the cartilage of the student's mind that goes; Rich if from a rich man's school, Poor if from a poor man's school. Four; They just really, honestly, want to know what school you'd transferred from.

"I'd rather not say," and with that Jeremy, as he'd come to be associated with, scribbled his way to the very back of the crowded class.

I let out a breath I didn't realize I had been holding. The aura and countenance to him felt completely out of the usual. I'm not the boy's type, neither am I the friendly type.

Screw that.

I used to be so friendly. I used to be much so friendly. Maybe a bit too friendly.

There was a time when I was this easygoing fun-filled person, when I used to feel things, when I could feel love and love back in return, when I was happy. However, the term 'was' is used to refer to past actions.

But then, Jeremy. I couldn't stop thinking about him throughout the day. I was constantly turning back, trying to catch a full view of him. I had seen that exact expression before. I had been well acquainted with the stories behind it, and they're not quite the pleasant type.

They were the type of stories that make one lose belief in things they had believed firmly in. They were the type of stories that made even the brightest of colors fade slowly into the harsh shadows of the night, never to regain their vibrancy.

The Black Gate

It was 12:00pm. She knew she should be right then inside her home, in the comfort of her bed. She knew that the last place she’d wanna be was here. She crept slyly like a creature of the night under the bright illuminating glare of the full moon as she casted shadow on the rocky ground. She shuffled quickly into the shadows of what happen to be, as she turned around a wide black gate.

The gate! She was met with a climax of alternate feelings. On one hand she was glad she’d finally found it, on the other hand she was scared she’d actually found it! Every ounce in her body screamed for her to run. And run, she was about to when she heard shuffling from the bushes to the path which she had just taken. The shuffling stopped just as soon as an even more upsetting sound of an engine took off. And right before her eyes emerged a Truck, from the blues of the bushes with headlights shining bright head-on. She ducked quickly and went to the far end of the gate where the path ended.

Her heart now beat loudly and haphazardly as she watched the Truck successfully curve round the path out bushes with it’s back towards her some feet away. When the lights of the Truck went off and the door of the Truck clicked open, Asha jumped up slightly and dropped the torchlight in her hands, as it made a noise. She cursed silently underneath her breath.

“Who’s there!!!”, a male voice called out. It sounded harsh but the little tremble in the voice couldn’t be missed.
