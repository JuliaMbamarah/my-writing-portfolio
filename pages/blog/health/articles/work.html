5 Ways to Become More Productive When Working from Home

Date: November 1, 2023 | Author: Julia Mbamarah

Working from home was not so popularly practiced by us until the COVID-19 pandemic hit the globe. This era affected virtually everything that you and I did ranging from tourism, work, traveling, sports, religious activities, meetings and conferences to mention but a few. Industries, companies, and institutions from all spheres of life had no option but to work remotely. You are well aware that working from home has now become the order of the day as well as the new future of work.

Working remotely comes with so many benefits, from having a comfortable, flexible schedule and a lot more. However, working from home can be quite tricky and dangerous if not properly managed. Procrastination and distractions are some of the ills that accompany working remotely. You will need to put in extra effort to be more productive while working from home. Here are five ways to become more productive when working from home.

#Tip 1 - CREATE AN OFFICE OR WORKSPACE AT HOME

Providing a working area at home is a very crucial step in achieving productivity while working from home. Select a space within your home, put a good working desk, chair, computer with a good internet connection that will be comfortable to work with; however, it mustn’t be so comfortable so as to distract you or make you slack during work. This is actually the spot where you do all your work.

Keep your workspace neat and tidy with great natural or artificial lighting that is appealing to the eyes. Endeavor to stuff your workspace with the necessary working materials to enhance focus and productivity while working remotely. A gentle reminder, using your bed or couch as your workspace will only hamper your productivity.

#Tip 2 - PREPARE A TO-DO LIST

Having a to-do list helps you to checkmate yourself on the things you need to accomplish and also enables visualizing and monitoring your progress. Personally, I have found this very helpful because whenever I start to digress from what I have set out to do, my to-do list will always put me back on track. Make your to-do list time-bound. Endeavor to strike out each project and work once they are accomplished. This will invariably help in monitoring your work progress.

At the end of each working day, outline the next day’s to-do list to mentally prepare you for the next task or project ahead. If you fail to plan, you plan to fail.

#Tip 3 - AVOID PROCRASTINATION

I bet we all fall prey to this kanker worm that eats up our working time which leads to low productivity. To be on the safe side, always try to accomplish every task in your to-do list at the exact time allocated for it. Never leave any stone unturned.

I know you may be thinking, “What if I do 85% of my designated work and then complete the rest tomorrow?” No is definitely my honest answer. You will develop the habit of constantly pushing things off to another day which is definitely difficult to get out of.

Procrastination can really put you back further during days of emergency. This will interrupt your work and finally lead to low productivity which is quite different from the set goal. Working from home requires great discipline to ensure great productivity.

#Tip 4 - CONSTANT COMMUNICATION AND CONNECTION

Communication is very paramount and important if you are to attain great productivity when working from home. Communicating with your supervisor, boss, team, or client regularly goes a long way to reflect positively on your work ethics. Always keep your boss, supervisor, or client updated on what you are working on and reach out to your team when a need for help and assistance arises.

If you are confused about the project you are handling and you need clarification, communication with the appropriate channel will certainly do justice to it. Endeavor to have a total understanding of the task or project you are working on and try to communicate any form of misconception to the right people be it your supervisor, your boss, your team, or your client. This will facilitate the desired productivity while working from home.

#Tip 5 - HAVE INTERMITTENT BREAKS

As the saying goes “All work and no play makes Jack a dull boy.” The play in this context is having intermittent breaks when working from home. Although you will be working ceaselessly hard, following your schedule and making sure you accomplish your set goals for the day, take some time off to really take care of yourself.

It’s very necessary to take a few brief breaks throughout the day and have a power nap, you could take a snack break, talk to a loved one, meditate, take a stroll, walk your dog and have so many other relaxation activities. It is quite tricky to feel like you could be online and available at all hours of the workday. As long as you are not away from your workspace for more than 20 minutes, it is appropriate and advisable to have intermittent breaks when working remotely.

Working remotely is very different from working from the office. Working from home certainly requires discipline. Always be intentional in order to be productive. You will find working from home a lot easier if you follow the tips. The key is to keep the best work balance, instill discipline, establish boundaries and finally take very good care of yourself.
