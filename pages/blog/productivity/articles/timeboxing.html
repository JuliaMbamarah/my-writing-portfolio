The Ultimate Guide to Time Boxing for Busy People

Date: November 1, 2023 | Author: Julia Mbamarah

The Ultimate Guide to Time Boxing for Busy People

With such little time left after the daily hustling and bustling of a day, work may sometimes seem overwhelming. Most people cannot remember when last they had free time or thought about something that wasn’t work-related.

If you can relate to this, then you might have fallen into the trap that Parkinson described.

According to him, “Work expands so as to fill the time available for its completion”. This means that even if you have little work to do and have say five days to complete it, it may take you the entire 5 days to complete the work. If, on the other hand, you have just 4 hours to complete that same work, you would eventually succeed in completing the job under just four hours.

Most people are stuck in the first reality, and they don’t seem to understand why. It’s one thing to be busy, and it’s another entirely to be ‘busy wasting time’. This is also the reason why someone with a low productivity level may seem busier than someone with a high productivity level.

But why is this so and how what can you do about it? Well, let’s talk about Time boxing.

What is Time boxing

Time boxing is where you’ll get to realize that you’ve probably been spending a lot more time than is necessary to complete different tasks. As the saying goes, you cannot evaluate what you cannot measure. If you do not intentionally plan and allocate time to the different tasks you do, then you cannot properly evaluate your productivity.

Time boxing is a form of time management where you allocate a fixed maximum amount of time for an activity or a task and then proceed to complete that activity within the set time frame. It typically follows an Allocate-Work-Stop-Access-Repeat pattern.

You allocate a fixed and reasonable period of time for an activity, you work on that activity within the time block, once the time is up, you stop, then you review your tasks and assess whether you have reached your time goal. You can always repeat this process and record your progress. This is how you measure your productivity.

There are several steps to follow if you want to adequately master this art of time boxing. This will save you a lot of time whilst giving you the ability to track your progress and realize your actual time span for different tasks.

How to Adequately Timebox

Step 1 – List out activities to be completed

To adequately allocate time to each of your activities, you need to list them out so you can see a visual representation of all the activities that you need to complete. This can help you categorize your tasks into short or long tasks, urgent or pending tasks, demanding or simple tasks.

The ability to categorize your tasks will help you to understand how these tasks differ and the different time blocks that you should assign to each of them. Start with listing out the difficult tasks first, and then proceed to list out the ones that can be done within a few minutes. Remember not to leave out a single activity.

Step 2 – Define your goals

You need to clearly define the goals that are associated with each and every one of your tasks. Your goal may be result-oriented. For example, if you were assigned to create a PowerPoint for a group presentation, then your goal may be something in the line of- “I want to have a visually appealing presentation with 10 slides that have been edited with Microsoft PowerPoint. And if your goal is time-bound, then it may be something in the line of “I want to have a visually appealing presentation with 10 slides that have been edited with Microsoft PowerPoint by the end of the day.”

Properly defining your goals and not leaving any detail behind will enable you to effectively plan your activities without leaving any stone unturned.

Step 3 – Set the time

This is where time management comes in. You can draw up a table manually or with a computer, or you can decide to use a time management app. You basically want something that can show you the different days of the week in a complete 24-hours time frame.

After you have clearly defined your goals, it’s time to start allocating time to them, that is, the number of hours you’re willing to work on them daily and the total amount of time it would take you to successfully complete a project. This process is called time boxing, and it allows you to allot a fixed, maximum unit of time for activities and then complete them within that time frame.

Step 4 – Work and Review

The next step is to do the actual work while adhering to all the time frames that you have set for yourself. You want to stick to the plan so that you can accurately capture your progress. Start with your first scheduled task and follow it exactly as you have planned it. It's just as the saying goes, ‘Half of the work is in the planning'. This way, you juggle so many tasks at once or worry about the next activity to do.




I hope this guide has been helpful. If you find yourself drowning in all your activities, then timeboxing might just be the approach that you’ve been looking for. You can adopt it in your daily routine and use it to track your activities, measure your productivity, and realize more time for yourself and your schedule.
