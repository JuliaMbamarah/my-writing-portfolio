Why Buy Gold? 10 Reasons to Invest in Gold in 2023

Date: November 1, 2023 | Author: Julia Mbamarah

With the recent boom in digital currencies and the wide diversification of investment opportunities, most people just can’t help being confused about what to invest in. More opportunities for investments mean more skepticism and confusion towards which kind of investments to engage in. Well, that is totally normal because the more opportunities for investments are out there, the more work that is to be done to follow up the investment’s history, trends, new developments as well as their future implication.

The same issue presents itself to people who want to invest in gold are unsure of where to start, whether investing in gold is a viable option or even what gold stock to buy. Gold was highly valued as far back as 4000 BC for how attractive and lustrous it was. It was a symbolism for power, wealth, and beauty. Found in its natural state as a shiny metal, it was earlier used for decorative and ornamental purposes because of its physically attractive nature and was eventually given spiritual symbolism to Gods and great rulers. However, the ancients soon found out that those were just a fraction of what gold is used for and in the 1500’s BC, gold became a medium of exchange for international trades.

Gold has a dense yellow lustre, denoted by AU from the word Aurum and belongs to period 6 and group 11 of the periodic table. It is durable and highly malleable and can be obtained in nature in its pure form. The amount of gold in the world can be wrapped eleven times around the world if it was pulled into a five-micron thick wire.

Interesting History

The ‘Mint and Coinage Act’ that was passed by the US congress in 1792 was probably one of the biggest factors that drove the gold investment market. However, Gold as an investment came into existence in 1970 when the US government yet again made legal the trading of physical Gold and reversed it after five years with the Gold Reverse Act of 1934. This occurred during the great depression where some countries like the US and the UK abandoned the gold standard.

Gold as a financial investment

It is without doubt that gold has been a very valued commodity for ages and an investment opportunity with proven track records and a lot of opportunities for the future. The key here lies in understanding the market, the economics that drives this commodity, when best to invest in gold and even what gold stock to buy.

But, will gold price increase this 2021? This is the question that a lot of people have asked and are still asking and it is for this reason that we would explore 10 reasons why we must invest in gold this 2021:

1. Coverage against Deflation

The great Depression of the 1930’s is always a great example to cite because there was a global price decrease and slowness of business activities. At this time, because of the general insecurity about currencies dropping, people exchanged their money for gold. What this did was to protect the value and even skyrocketed it as the value of currencies dropped across borders. Deflation is a problem that is repeating and that is why to protect money value, gold is the best option to turn to. In recent light, the Covid-19 pandemic that hit the world in 2020 saw the global economy shutting down and most countries' currency value dropping with light’s speed. Now check out how the price of gold performed through it all.

2. Portfolio Multiplicity

Having a diversified investment is a great way of reducing volatility associated with investments. Diversified in this sense refers to investing in a commodity that is not correlated to other stocks and if any commodity possesses this quality, it is gold. This is proven through the historical records of gold especially in the late 1970’s and early 2000’s where the market was favorable for gold but horrible for other stocks. Conversely, in the 1980’s, gold suffered while other stocks thrived. The independency of gold is probably one of the major reasons why you should invest in gold right now.

3. Pace of Gold Production

There has been a constraint to the global supply of gold from central banks since the 2000s and a decline in the rate of production from gold mines. Ordin Ordinarily, a reduction in the production of gold leads to a subsequent increase in price and affects how often gold price changes. This would keep increasing with the estimated decrease in the production of gold.

4. Intergovernmental Incertitude

In times of geopolitical uncertainty and economic instability, gold is a run-to haven because of its known ability to retain its value, especially during times of crisis. Countries experience geopolitical problems every now and then, and even if your country is not experiencing any instability, gold is still a great option for you to save your money because now, there are no uncertainties.

5. Inflation Barrier

Just as gold protects currencies from deflation, it acts as a hedge against inflation as the price increases when the cost of living increases. It is a good store of value, especially when people in a country perceive that their currency is losing value.

6. Value Holding History

Unlike any other commodity, gold has a strong history of maintaining and even increasing its value. It is even seen as a means of wealth transfer between generations as it doesn’t fade away or corrode.

7. Measurable Asset

It is a tangible asset in the sense that it gives a safety feeling as compared to purchasing an asset like real estate and also because unlike other digital assets, it cannot be hacked so security is assured.

8. Rising Demand amongst Investors

With demand rising amongst stockholders and new investors as the potential of gold as a financial investment is being recognized, the price of gold is increasing.

9. Dollar Strength

When the prices of the dollar fluctuate, people tend to flock to the security of gold and this, in turn, increases the price of gold.

10. Easily Accessible Rate of Liquidation

It has a fast rate of liquidation as compared to other commodities; It has no lock-in period and can easily be redeemed.
