7 Tips to Help you stop procrastinating on Social media

Date: November 1, 2023 | Author: Julia Mbamarah

While some people will assure you that they’ve never had to deal with procrastination as they love to get their things done in time, a lot of people like you and me struggle with procrastination.


When you think about the tasks you have to complete, your brain unconsciously weighs it’s gravity and over exaggerates the outcome to your feeble mind. To get rid of the overwhelming feeling, your brain offers you a lighter solution- social media, and then overestimates how good that momentary pleasure would make you feel. After that you realize that your work is still undone and run into a panicked frenzy.

In fact, you end up in a down spiraling cycle of anxiety, temporal relief and unproductivity. Definitely not a pleasant experience.

The good thing is you never have to go through that ever again.

If you struggle with procrastination, I have compiled a list of 7 Tips to help you stop procrastinating on social media.

#Tip 1 – Put the device down!

-and before you come for me, yes. I know it’s a digital world and the work you have to do is literally on your phone or your computer, however you can still put your phone down in the literal sense. Ideally, I recommend having a different device for work related activities and a different one for extracurricular activities. But since everyone may not have the luxury, you can do simple but effective things like turning off all social media notifications, and blocking off unimportant calls for the duration of the task to be completed.





#Tip 2 – Change your environment

I bet you didn’t think of this one but this would literally work wonders for you. Your brain is wired in such a way that it associates location with activities. If you spend most of your time on social media when you’re lying down on your bed in your room, then chances are that the next time you’re in your room, your mind would subconsciously crave to achieve a similar effect. So don’t be surprised if you’re trying to do your work project and you suddenly find yourself scrolling away through your social media.

Have a study room or a library that is only meant for work and this way, the only thing your brain can associate with when you’re in this room is work.





#Tip 3 – Outline your plans

Do you know that complications and procrastination are best friends? Those two walk hand in hand. You’re more likely to eagerly complete a simple task than a difficult one. When the brain feels like a task would be too stressful and demanding on it, it shuts downs. Sneaky little thing!

The best way to approach this is to break down the tasks you have to do into little parts. Asides making the brain now willing to analyze your task, it makes your work simpler! Next time you have an important work, remember that half of it’s progress lies in trying to break down the work into an outline. You can make up to 6 or 7 milestone just as long as they are able to capture every major tasks.





#Tip 4 – Create Timelines

Don’t just stop at making outlines for your plans. Add timelines to them as well. Do you know the effect this has? It makes you accountable for your plans. When you’ve set out to accomplish a list of things within different time frame, you’ll be more eager to complete each task in time as you would not want to encroach into another task’s timeline.

When I have chores or projects to do, I ensure to create a list of how I would like to complete the tasks and then assign timelines to them. The best thing about this is that you can do it all on your terms, so long as you get your task completed and in time.





#Tip 5 – Take breaks

If you try to overexert yourself, your body will shutdown and will make sure you do not succeed in your quest to adequately complete your tasks.

Be sure to include minor breaks in between tasks when creating your outline. This will make your plan less discouraging and much more easier to follow. This is also a way of applauding yourself for completing major milestones in your assignments or projects.





#Tip 6 – Share your plans

Unless you plan on doing something wrong, then maybe don’t share it; however, If you want to get something done quickly then just share it and you’ll never hear the end of it. If you share your plans with people in the same space as you like your family and friends, then chances are that they will keep checking in with you to see if you were finally able to complete what you talked to them about.





#Tip 7- Take advantage of the power of now

Lastly, if you’re already at that point where you feel miserable for not doing your work, and you have little time left, then just do this.

Start now. You need to take advantage of the power of now. This simply means that, right now, you should stop reading this article and drop your phone. You need to weigh the gravity of your work, the time you have left and with all the tips we have listed above, plan towards making productive use of the remaining time. You’d most likely achieve a far better result than you would have regretting your decisions.



I’m really glad that we had this talk. If you follow this tips then you can be rest assured that you will never have to worry about trading your project or assignments for social media ever again.
