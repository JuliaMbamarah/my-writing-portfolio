How to Pick Quality website for Guest posting: 15 Metrics to check

Date: November 1, 2023 | Author: Julia Mbamarah

Do not make the mistake that most people make in their quest to find a guest blog site. The most popular question I get is “What sites do I pick for guest posting?” Guest blogging is one of the smartest ways to get your name out there as a blogger. You write articles for a guest blog that contains links that direct back to your own blog. Since it requires a lot of time and effort, it can be frustrating when done wrong.

Here are several metrics to check in picking quality websites for guest posting:

#One – The site’s relevancy to your business niche

A big waste of your time as well as energy would be pitching to sites whose content is unrelated to your niche. This is a common mistake amongst new bloggers who want to build a backlink profile. You must navigate the website and find out if its goals, content as well as the target audience align with that of your own website.

#Two – The site’s credibility

Another important metric to put into consideration before choosing a website is its credibility. Is the website credible? Reliable? Consider navigating through the landing pages of the website to see its "About Us" section and see if it sends a strong message. Navigate through its mission as well as visions. Ensure also that the website looks updated and well put together.

#Three – The site’s traffic

There are several tools you can use to track the traffic that a website gets. A factor you should look out for is the monthly visit that the site gets. The higher this is, the greater the chances are for your blog to be viewed by more people and the more the referral traffic that would be generated.

#Four – The site’s affiliated blog

There should be a blog associated with any website that you’re going to write guest posts for and you need to be sure of this. If there are no associated blogs, then your content cannot be posted, and you would have wasted your time and energy writing guest posts for that website.

#Five – The site’s webpage design

There are several aspects of the website design you should look out for that are indicators of what the website can offer. Look out for the site's professional design, its unique custom-designed images, the ease of navigation, its mobile responsiveness, the number of pops and ads, and lastly, the number of low-quality external links associated with the website.

#Six – The site’s SEO metrics

You need to properly verify the SEO metrics of a site to see if it can contribute positively to your site's SEO. There are about 200 ranking factors to optimize on your site. You should look for sites with high domain authority, low spam score, high Moz rank, high trust flow, high citation flow, high organic ranking, and domain ranking.

#Seven – The site’s published contents

An important step to take before you pitch any article for guest blogging is to check previously published contents on the website. This is necessary for you to get an ideal feel for the kind of content that they post, the ideal word length, their writing style, article format, content quality, writing tone, and their use of unique images.

#Eight - The site’s social influence

If you want to know the quality of a site that you want to choose for your guest posting, then a very good criterion to check is its social media outreach. The metrics that you want to check include: the number of page following, its engagements on its social platforms, its active engagers, its frequency of posts, and its content niche.

#Nine – The site’s backlink’s issuing conditions

Something you may want to check is how strict the site is in giving backlinks. It is good to know all the conditions associated with the links you provide on the site. A good rule of thumb is to check if guest post links back to your site or are just author bio links. Check how long the sites will put up backlinks before taking them down.

#Ten - The domain history

You need to check if the website domain is expired. An expired domain is a domain that has not been renewed and it can be dangerous when used to sell links. To find out if a website has an expired domain, there are several tools that you can use to analyze its historical footprint. Try to avoid such websites.

#Eleven - Avoid PBNs – Private blog networks

Just like expired domains, using PBNs to generate backlinks can have dire consequences. PBNs are like link pyramids or networks. A network of blogs is created to keep exchanging backlinks. This has a way of manipulating the SEO ranking of the site. A penalty is being associated with such acts by Google, and so it is neither worth the time nor the energy.

#Twelve – The quality of their backlinks

There are several tools that you can use to check the quality of the backlinks for websites that you’re going to be pitching for. You need to check if they have high-quality as well as reputable links and even brand mentions from some big and reputable sites. Try to avoid sites that feature backlinks that are spammy as well as valueless.

#Thirteen - Articles Indexing Style

Before you write your articles for guest blogging, you should ensure that Google indexes the webpage in a timely fashion. Start by viewing the website's most recent article, and then google the title of the article together with the site search operator. This process should be repeated to understand how articles get indexed.

#Fourteen - Guesting post fee

Note that some opportunities may cost you in order to get your articles to the editor’s eyes. There may be fees associated with posting publications for some sites. Do make sure that the site in question has excellent authority and engagements accruing to it. In a situation whereby the site does not pass this check and still requests payment, you should pass on that opportunity.

#Fifteen - The ratio of outgoing-to-incoming domains

An important factor to check is the ratio of the outgoing to the incoming backlinks on the sites. The website receives inbound links from other sites like yours while outbound links are pages that the website links to. The number of inbound links is more important on the website because SEO values diversity.

It is important for you as a blogger, hoping to get blog posting opportunities to carefully apply the entire matrix as listed. This would save a whole lot of time and energy and would give you the required SEO ranking as well as engagement on your site. It pays to take time to select which website that you should send your pitch to.

So the next time you send that pitch, make sure you’ve done your assignment and can choose the best website to link your content.
